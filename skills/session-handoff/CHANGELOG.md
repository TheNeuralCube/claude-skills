# Changelog

All notable changes to the session-handoff skill will be documented in this file.

The format is based on [Keep a Changelog](https://keepachangelog.com/en/1.1.0/), and this skill adheres to [Semantic Versioning](https://semver.org/spec/v2.0.0.html).

**Schema and skill versions are decoupled.** The skill version (e.g. `0.1.0`) tracks releases. The schema version (`schema_version` in every handoff's frontmatter) bumps only when the shape of the metadata contract changes. The skill ships at v0.1.0 and the schema ships at "0.1" by coincidence, not by coupling: a future patch release that touches no contract field still writes `schema_version: "0.1"`. See `references/schema-changelog.md` for the contract's version history.

## [0.1.0] - 2026-06-02

Genesis public release. session-handoff is the public, brand-free successor to an internal v1.5 predecessor skill, renamed because the output is a forward transfer optimized for a future AI agent, not a backward recap.

### Added

- **Five modes behind one auto-routed entry point.** generate (carried forward from v1.5), update, consolidate, retrofit (carried forward from v1.5), and share-sanitize. A mandatory pre-flight check detects prior handoffs via the `_managed_by: session-handoff-skill` marker, classifies the input, and proposes one operation with a verdict and a confirmation token before any write. Routing table and verdicts in `references/preflight.md`; mode logic in `modes/`.
- **The `## Protocol` structural gate.** SKILL.md's `## Protocol` section is the first content after frontmatter; the model cannot reach mode content without passing through pre-flight. Block absence is a release-blocking bug. Mirrors the project-context protocol-enforcement pattern.
- **Two-tier metadata contract at schema "0.1".** A tight, versioned metadata contract (`references/schema.md`) is separated from a mode-coupled resumption payload (owned by the mode files). The contract carries identity, lineage, governance, a `generated_by` provenance block, and an `audit` block. A no-empty-fields rule makes an empty required field a Parse Error.
- **Two-zone, machine-parseable resumption payload (carried in shape from v1.5).** Zone 1 is structured YAML (the tier-1 contract plus the tier-2 payload blocks: `project`, `artifacts` with `state_markers` and `safe_edit_rules`, `schemas`, `state_snapshot`, `decisions`, `known_issues`, `open_items`, `continuation`, `changelog`, and optional `people_involved`/`tags`/`guardrails_summary`); Zone 2 is the narrative body that expands on Zone 1. The canonical field-by-field payload schema is owned by `modes/generate.md`. This preserves the machine-parseable payload that made the predecessor useful: artifact state, verifiable metrics, and the cold-start playbook are structured, not prose.
- **`references/section-activation.md` (new).** The Zone 2 narrative section set, conditional activation by `thread_type` and content signal, the fixed section order, and the writing rules (third-person observer voice, no emphasis formatting, no tables in narrative, reference deliverables by exact filename, no em dashes). Includes a **Source Ingestion Context** section gated on `thread_type: research` or any data source processed this session: an operator-approved scope addition beyond the locked design block set, documented in an accompanying design-spec delta for the Hub to ratify.
- **Self-contained lineage.** `handoff_id` (derived `HND-<topic_slug>-<YYYYMMDD>-s<NN>`, replacing the predecessor's id prefix), `handoff_version`, `supersedes`, `prior_handoffs`, `consolidation_depth`, and `derivative_of` are all defined in-contract. The predecessor's external lookup table is removed; all identity lives in-document. Supersession, consolidation identity, most-restrictive governance propagation, and governance-gated source retention live in `references/lineage.md`.
- **The update length contract.** A per-version soft budget in tokens (default 12000, destination-aware), with current-versus-resolved curation: live items carry at full fidelity, items resolved since the prior version compress to one-line ledger entries recoverable in the retained prior version. It surfaces a verdict on overflow; it never hard-blocks. A lightweight heuristic, explicitly not a scoring algorithm or merge classifier. `references/defaults.md`, `modes/update.md`.
- **Consolidation identity and the cap.** A consolidation mints a new identity, builds a `prior_handoffs` ledger, derives governance most-restrictively from its sources, and sets `consolidation_depth`. A soft source-count cap (default 3) guards review quality; the per-version budget is the technical ceiling. `references/lineage.md`, `modes/consolidate.md`.
- **share-sanitize: the seam, not the engine.** A built-in, model-driven redactor produces a redacted leaf derivative (`derivative_of` set, `generation_mode: sanitized`) plus a manifest of categories and counts (never content) with a mandatory assistive disclaimer. Behind a `redaction_provider` switch reserving a future `document-sanitizer` sister skill. `references/redaction.md`, `modes/share-sanitize.md`.
- **The governance framework.** A governance block on every handoff (sensitivity, retention, frameworks, custom), a sensitivity-aware receiving-agent handling block, sanctioned-destination awareness, and most-restrictive consolidation propagation. Eight enterprise features ship in the core, calibrated by config. `references/governance.md`.
- **The two honesty guardrails.** PII flagging is assistive and never claims completeness; `approved_by` is self-asserted and not platform-verified. Stated in `references/schema.md`, `references/governance.md`, and `README.md`.
- **The config-layer convention.** A dedicated `config/` directory for editable files (`user-config.md.template`, `org-config.md.template`, `redaction-policy.md.template`, and the shipped `platform-parameters.md`), a single shared `references/configure.md` interview, self-documenting AI-walkable config files with a standard header and local field guides, and approval-diff discipline (never silent-overwrite). Config files auto-create from templates on first run. Proposed as a cross-skill standard; project-context invited to converge in v0.7.0.
- **Surface awareness, never decline.** The skill notes that Claude Code and Cowork yield higher model-identity provenance but never declines a surface. This inverts the project-context surface guard, which is deliberately not adopted.
- **Decoupled schema versioning and a schema-changelog.** `references/schema-changelog.md` carries the Supported Schemas matrix and the 0.1 inception entry, and states the schema-versus-skill decoupling loudly.
- **README, USAGE, ROADMAP.** Full customization instructions (layered config, redaction posture, when-to-use versus project-context), per-mode walkthroughs including a plain-language share-sanitize explanation, and deferred items.

### Provenance

- **Carried forward from an internal v1.5 predecessor skill:** the generate and retrofit logic, adapted to the new two-tier schema. v1.5 is the seed; this is a renamed, brand-stripped, dependency-decoupled public release.
- **Removed from the core:** the predecessor's brand prefix, its two internal dependencies (a naming-and-versioning conventions skill and a capture-pipeline skill), and all operator-specific and organization-specific behavior. Naming and dotted-semver versioning are inlined rather than depending on the retired conventions skill. The brand-stripped v1.5 fields (`aspect`, `confidence`, `last_verified`, `thread_chain`, `vault_path`, `parent_moc`, `aliases`, `related_notes`, `life_domains`) move to a separately-packaged org-config profile. See `references/schema-changelog.md` for the full field disposition.
- **Org-specific variant:** a separately-packaged variant layers the public core with an org-config profile that wires organization sequencing, capture hooks, org naming and frontmatter fields, and `sanitization_prompt: always`. Packaged after the public push; no org-specific behavior lives in the public core.

### Notes

- **Why 0.1.0 and not 1.0.0.** The generate and retrofit cores are mature (carried from v1.5), but update, consolidate, and share-sanitize are unproven. v1.0.0 is not yet earned. The build session is the first field test of the three new modes; their identity, lineage, governance-propagation, and content-preservation behavior were treated as build-blocking.
- **No em dashes** appear anywhere in the skill or its generated output, by design.
- **Honesty guardrails are permanent constraints, not v0.1.0 limitations.** They persist until a platform identity API and a proven redaction engine exist.
